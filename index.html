▼ About Me

About Me

I’m a builder at heart. Over the past two decades, I’ve had the privilege of working on foundational technologies— from ActiveSync, speech recognition, and Windows at Microsoft, to Marketplace at Meta, and more recently, AI-powered data protection at Nightfall.

My career has taken me from Morocco to the heart of Silicon Valley, and I’ve learned that the most meaningful progress happens when deep technology meets human empathy. I’m driven by impact—solving problems that matter, at scale.

I’ve led product and engineering teams in large companies, currently learning and enjoying the thrills of small startups. I have learned from and collaborated with incredible people along the way. Above all, I consider myself a hands-on builder and a lifelong learner, energized by what’s next and grounded in the belief that big things start small.

Whether mentoring, building, or investing, I aim to empower others to lead boldly, build wisely, and never wait for permission to make a difference.

▼ Reflections

Ideas & Insights

I started writing publicly to reflect on the journey that shaped me — from Morocco to Microsoft, from engineering to leadership, and from quietly building to mentoring and giving back.

This blog isn’t about being an expert. It’s about being real. I believe that sharing our stories — especially the messy, in-between parts — helps others feel less alone, and reminds us that growth isn’t linear.

Whether you're an aspiring engineer, a founder figuring things out, or simply someone who’s ever felt like they didn’t quite belong, I hope you’ll find something here that resonates.

My only goal is to contribute with intention. And if even one post helps someone take their next step with confidence, then it’s already worth it.

I’ll be writing soon — stay tuned for reflections on tech, leadership, and impact.
